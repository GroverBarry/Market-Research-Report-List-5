<p><p>Plasma separation systems are commonly categorized into two main types: membrane separation and centrifugal separation. Membrane separation systems utilize semipermeable membranes to separate particles from plasma based on molecular size, while centrifugal systems rely on spinning components to separate plasma components based on their density. Both types of systems are widely used in various industries such as healthcare, biotechnology, and research, offering efficient and reliable methods for separating plasma components for further analysis or processing.</p></p>
