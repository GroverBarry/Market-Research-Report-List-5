<p><p>Automotive high-performance brake systems primarily come in two types: disc brakes and drum brakes. Disc brakes are more commonly used in high-performance vehicles due to their superior stopping power and heat dissipation capabilities. They consist of a rotor and caliper that work together to apply pressure and slow down the vehicle. On the other hand, drum brakes are simpler in design and are often found in rear wheel applications. Both types of brake systems are essential in ensuring safe and efficient braking performance in high-performance vehicles.</p></p>
