<p><p>Dental bone graft and substitutes market types include allograft, autograft, xenograft, and synthetic options. Allografts are bone grafts taken from a donor of the same species, while autografts are taken from the patient's own body. Xenografts are sourced from a different species, typically bovine or porcine. Synthetic bone grafts are man-made materials designed to mimic the properties of natural bone. Each type has its own advantages and drawbacks, allowing for a range of options in dental bone graft procedures.</p></p>
