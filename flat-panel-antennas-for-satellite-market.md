<p><p>Flat panel antennas for the satellite market come in two main types: Electronically Controlled Antennas (ESA) and Mechanically Controlled Antennas (MSA). ESA utilize electronic components to adjust the antenna's beam direction, allowing for faster and more precise tracking of satellite signals. On the other hand, MSA use mechanical systems to physically move the antenna to track satellites, offering a more cost-effective alternative. Both types cater to different market needs, with ESA being more advanced and MSA being more economical.</p></p>
