<p><p>Phenolic biocides are commonly classified into three main types: nitrophenols, chlorophenols, and others. Nitrophenols are chemical compounds derived from phenol with a nitro group. Chlorophenols are compounds that contain both phenol and chlorine atoms. The "others" category includes various phenolic biocides that do not fall into the nitrophenols or chlorophenols categories. Each type of phenolic biocide has unique properties and applications in different industries such as agriculture, water treatment, and healthcare.</p></p>
