<p><p>Intermediate Shafts in the automotive market come in two main types - slidable and collapsible. Slidable intermediate shafts are designed to absorb vibrations and reduce noise by accurately adjusting the length of the shaft as needed. On the other hand, collapsible intermediate shafts are designed to collapse in the event of a crash, reducing the risk of injury to the driver and passengers by absorbing impact energy. Both types play a crucial role in ensuring smooth and safe operation of the vehicle's drivetrain.</p></p>
