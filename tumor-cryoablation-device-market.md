<p><p>Tumor cryoablation devices utilize different types of refrigeration systems for cooling purposes. The Phase Change Refrigeration system relies on the phase change of a refrigerant to achieve cooling, while the Joule-Thomson Throttling Refrigeration system uses the Joule-Thomson effect to cool gases as they expand through a throttling valve. Both systems are commonly used in tumor cryoablation devices to reach the ultra-low temperatures required to effectively freeze and destroy cancerous tissues. Each system has its own advantages and applications in the medical field.</p></p>
