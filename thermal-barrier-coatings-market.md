<p><p>Thermal barrier coatings are used to protect materials from extreme temperatures and corrosion. Metal/alloy coatings are typically made from materials like nickel, cobalt, and chromium, offering excellent resistance to heat and oxidation. Ceramic coatings, on the other hand, are composed of materials like zirconia and alumina, providing superior thermal insulation and erosion resistance. Both types of coatings serve as effective barriers against high temperatures, but metal/alloy coatings are more commonly used in industrial applications, while ceramic coatings are often found in aerospace and automotive industries.</p></p>
