<p><p>Internal trauma fixation devices are used to stabilize fractured bones during the healing process. Types of fixation devices include plates, screws, poles, wires & pins, fusion nails, and others. Plates are flat metal pieces used to hold bones in place. Screws are used to secure plates and hold bones together. Poles are intramedullary rods inserted into bones for stability. Wires & pins are used for temporary fixation. Fusion nails are inserted into bones to promote fusion. Other fixation devices include external fixators and intramedullary nails.</p></p>
