<p><ul><li>Ion Exchange Membrane Method</li><li>Diaphragm Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The chlor-alkali equipment market can be divided into two main types: Ion Exchange Membrane Method and Diaphragm Method. The Ion Exchange Membrane Method utilizes a membrane to separate the anode and cathode compartments, allowing for the production of chlorine, caustic soda, and hydrogen. On the other hand, the Diaphragm Method uses a porous diaphragm to prevent mixing of the anolyte and catholyte, enabling the production of the same chemicals. These different methods cater to diverse needs and preferences within the chlor-alkali industry.</p></p>
